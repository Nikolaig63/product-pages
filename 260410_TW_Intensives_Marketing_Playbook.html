The Core Principle

You need twelve
conversations.
Not twelve thousand impressions.

The four decision types where untested assumptions are most expensive—AI deployments, growth bets, market entries, and transformation initiatives—are what the participant is navigating when they read the anchor article. The marketing programme exists to find those people systematically, connect the industry pattern to their live decision, and make attendance easy to justify before the decision commits. At 20 participants per cohort this is a precision problem, not a volume problem.

The underlying argument—from the book

The Think Wrong book opens with a neuroscience argument: the brain’s efficiency—synaptic pruning, the formation of shortcuts—is also its greatest obstacle to original thinking. Biology and culture conspire together to make the predictable path feel like the rational one. The six Think Wrong practices are specific counterpunches to specific moments where this happens. The marketing programme should reflect this argument, not just describe the programmes. The prospect who recognises their own organisation in the status quo framing is already converted. The prospect who reads a programme description is not. The book is now in its third printing (2025), published in English, Spanish, and Arabic. When a prospect asks whether this is a real methodology with real traction, the answer is: a decade old, three printings, three languages, Genentech, White House, Airbus. That is a complete answer.

(1) Frame the right challenge before solutions take hold. (2) Distinguish knowledge from belief for any decision. AI Enablement

Six AI layers.
All in service of
better conversations.

AI in this programme does not replace human judgment or personal credibility. It removes the friction that limits the number of high-quality conversations Greg and Louise can have. Each layer has a specific job and a specific tool.

AI Layer 01
Prospect Scoring & Signal Detection
Clay + Claude scans LinkedIn activity, company news, and earnings calls to find prospects who are actively grappling with the programme’s challenge—right now. Outputs a ranked list with a ‘why this person today’ paragraph per contact. A person who posted about EV transition challenges last week is a warmer Automotive prospect than someone whose profile just says “VP Strategy.”
AI Layer 02
Personalised First-Touch Drafting
Once prospect intelligence exists, Claude drafts a personalised first-touch message for each contact in Greg or Louise’s voice—referencing their specific recent post, their company’s specific challenge, or a shared connection’s context. Human reviews and approves each one. Drafting time drops from 15 minutes to 90 seconds per message. That is the difference between 5 outreaches a day and 30.
AI Layer 03
Trigger-Based Outreach Monitoring
Automated monitoring for signals that indicate a prospect is ready: company restructuring announcement, relevant LinkedIn post, industry report publication, earnings call mention of the challenge. When a trigger fires, outreach happens within 48 hours. Tools: Perplexity API for real-time monitoring, Make.com for workflow automation, Claude for classification and draft generation.
AI Layer 04
Content Repurposing at Scale
One well-written anchor article becomes every derivative format needed: LinkedIn post (Greg version), LinkedIn post (Louise version), email to the Solve Next list, five-slide sales summary, three provocation posts, and pull quotes from the fieldwork descriptions. Seven programmes × five formats = 35 pieces of content produced in a day rather than a month.
AI Layer 05
Conversation Intelligence
Exploratory calls are recorded (with consent), transcribed via Fireflies or Otter, and analysed by Claude. Output per call: the prospect’s specific assumption challenge in their own words, their escalation question answer verbatim, objections raised, and a follow-up email draft that reflects their own language back to them. A follow-up that quotes a prospect’s own words is categorically more effective than a generic one.
AI Layer 06
Programme Page Chatbot
A Claude-powered conversational widget on each programme page—trained on the programme content, methodology, pricing, and FAQ. Answers questions in the Solve Next voice at any hour. Ends every conversation with a direct path to [email protected] or a calendar link. Built using the Anthropic API with programme-specific system prompts. Converts late-night readers who have a question that would otherwise go unanswered.

Component 01  ·  Content

The anchor article.
Write it once.
Use it everywhere.

One 700-word article per programme. Not a brochure. Not a course description. A piece that makes a senior solution lead think “this is exactly what’s happening in my organisation.” The article ends with a single sentence pointing to the programme.

Structure
How to write the anchor article
Opening (100 words): A single observation about the industry that names the assumption failure pattern without using jargon. Specific, not general.

The pattern (300 words): Why this industry’s challenge is a structure that repeats across every industry. The reader should recognise their own organisation in a different sector’s story.

The implication (200 words): What happens to organisations that don’t surface the assumption before the capital commits. Concrete, not theoretical.

The close (100 words): One sentence naming the programme. One sentence naming the email.
Examples
Opening lines per programme
Wine: “The assumption that built the wine category is now the assumption that threatens it. This pattern has a name.”

Automotive: “Four forces arrived simultaneously at the UK automotive industry. It is solving three of them incorrectly.”

Drone Warfare: “A €500 FPV drone can destroy a €3 million asset. Most infrastructure operators have not updated their planning assumptions.”

Mobility: “Amsterdam solved the car problem a generation before anyone else. Now it is discovering that every solution embeds the next set of assumptions to fail.”

The identity layer—for closing paragraphs

Every anchor article should close with two sentences that do two jobs. First, name the four decision types explicitly: “If the AI deployment, growth bet, market entry, or transformation initiative you are navigating right now rests on premises you have not tested—” This is the pain. Second, offer the identity: “the conversation starts here.” The prospect who recognises their live decision in that sentence will contact you. The person who does not is not ready. That is the right filter.

Component 03  ·  Conversion

The cohort
mechanism.

The group rate is not a discount. It is a reframe that moves the decision from individual to organisational—which is easier to approve and more likely to fill the programme.

The offer in the conversation
After every qualified exploratory call that generates genuine interest, say: “If you think two or three colleagues would benefit—the group rate is €4,000 per person all-in including accommodation. Some organisations put this through as a team development initiative rather than an individual expense, which often makes the budget conversation easier.”

This is offered in every conversation without exception. It is the single highest-leverage action in the programme.
The escalation question as a conversion tool
At the end of every exploratory conversation—whether it goes well or not—ask:

“Of the assumptions we’ve talked about—which ones, if they turned out to be wrong, would change a decision that only your CFO or CEO can authorise?”

If the person can answer it, they are ready for the programme. Write the answer down. Reflect it back in the follow-up email. Connect it explicitly to what the week produces. This is where AI Layer 05 (conversation intelligence) earns its keep.
LFI—Learn From Investment
For L&D and manager approvals, reframe from ROI to LFI. “The question is not whether the week produces a measurable return. It is whether the assumptions we test are worth testing before the capital commits.” This comes directly from the Think Wrong book—which gives it authority. Use it whenever a prospect says the financial case is not clear enough.
AI Layer 05 in action
What the conversation intelligence output looks like
After the call transcript is processed, the AI produces four things: (1) the prospect’s specific assumption challenge in their own words, (2) their escalation question answer verbatim, (3) the hesitations or objections they raised, and (4) a follow-up email draft that opens with their own language reflected back at them. The human reviews, personalises the close, and sends. The prospect receives an email that demonstrates they were genuinely heard—which is the primary differentiator from every other programme they’ve been pitched.
